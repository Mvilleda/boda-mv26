Our wedding will take place in Atlixco, Puebla, about 2 hours from Mexico City. Here's how to get there:

ALREADY IN PUEBLA CITY?

You can take a local bus from CAPU station. Look for the ORO line. They leave every 30 minutes and tickets are around $45 MXN. Uber is also a great option if you prefer a direct ride.

FLYING IN

Most of you will fly into Mexico City (MEX) or Puebla (PBC). If you're coming from Cancún, you can fly directly to Puebla!

FROM MEXICO CITY (MEX)

The easiest route is to take the bus from the airport’s Terminal 2. You can book with ADO or Estrella Roja. Get off at Paseo Destino, then take an Uber to Atlixco.

FROM PUEBLA AIRPORT (PBC)

From Puebla airport, you can Uber directly to Atlixco. Renting a car is also an option if you bring a valid license.

If you need help planning your route, let us know. We are happy to help!

wHat To do In puebLa

You'll be spending time in one of Mexico's most beautiful corners, so make the most of it.

In Atlixco, take a stroll through the town centre, charming, unhurried, and full of colour. Don't miss Valle de las Catrinas, a spectacular sculptural celebration of Día de Muertos that takes over the town in late October.

A short drive away, Puebla city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site well worth a visit. Wander the historic centre, visit the Cathedral, explore the Callejón de los Sapos and El Parián, and stop at La Pasita for a shot of their famous liqueur. If you have more time, Los Fuertes de Loreto, the Museo Internacional del Barroco, and La Estrella de Puebla are all worth it. October is also a wonderful time to eat well. Don't leave without trying mole poblano, and ask around for mole de caderas, a regional dish served only in season.

In Cholula, the pyramid is unmissable. Explore it from the inside, then climb to the church perched at the top for sweeping views of Popocatépetl. Look out for the Voladores de Papantla performing in the plaza nearby.
